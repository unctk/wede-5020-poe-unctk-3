Our Programmes

Harnessing the power of accurate, timely, and transparent data to improve the quality of education across South Africa.

Data Driven Districts (DDD) Programme

The Data Driven Districts (DDD)programme is NLF's flagship initiative and is currently NLF's primary programme. It was launched in 2012 in partnership with the Michael & Susan Dell Founadation and the Department of Basic Educatioin (DBE).NLF serves as the implementation partner, providing support to national and provincial offices through training, capacity building, and tools for collecting and using quality education data.

The programme collects school-level data through the South african School Administration and Management System (SA-SAMS) and visualises it on the DDD Dashboard- a powerful, user-friendly managaement platform. The Dashboard provides quality, evidence-based insights on the drivers of poor performance, offering both micro and macro views:

  • Micro-level:Detailed individual learner performance information, enabling educators to affect change first-hand.
  • Macro-level:District performance information, allowing officials to assess issues impacting the broader local education landscape.

The DDD Dashboard currently represents over 11 million learners of South Africa's 12.5 million public school learners and is active in 40 districts across the country.

How the DDD Programme Works

Together with the Deparment of Basic Education, NLF regulary uncovers and indintifies important insight that redirects and refines purposeful and meaningful educational strategies to better learner outcomes. The programme therefore follows 4 key stages:

  1. Data Collecting:School grade data is put together from SA-SAMS across participating districts. This includes learner attendance, enrolment numbers, assessment results, and teacher deployment informaion.
  2. Data Processing:NLF's data processing team analyzes the collected data to identify trends, patterns, and areas for improvement. And validates the data for accuracy. and consistency before loading it into the DDD Dashboard.
  3. Insight Generation:The DDD Dashboard visualises the data in an easy-to-understand format. Providing both high level district summaries and drill down individual learner and district officals information.
  4. Action and Capacity Building:NLF Project Managers provide facilitated training and support to education officials across the allocated provinces, empowering them to act on data insights and create a data driven culture within their destricts

Programme Impact

Over the course of more than a decade of implementation, the DDD programme has demonstrated measurable improvements in the schools and districts it serves:

Improved Attendance

The DDD Dashboard has an early warning system that helps identify students that have been absent for more than 10 days, enabling timely interventions.

Subject Performance

Officals use the Dashboard to identify content knolwedge gaps and areas for improvement by per subject. In one case study, a focused Social Sciences intervention led to a marked improvement in subject results within a single term.

85% of Schools Covered

Through the DBE partnership, 85% of all schools in South Africa, and the districts, have access to the user friendly DDD Dashboard that provides information on learner performance and attendance including the teachers

Government Integration

The DDD programme is integrated with government systems to ensure seamless data flow and informed decision-making.This partnership has shown how a shared vision between government and the private sector can result in a scalable solution that fills a critical gap in the education system.

Shaping New Leaders

Beyond the DDD programme, NLF is committed to developing the next generation of education leaders from within its own organisation. NLF consistently hires graduates to work as New Leaders Consultants across seven provinces.

From external graduate opportunities to internal learner management systems and aligned study assistance, NLF is passionate about seeing talent and opportunity come together in pursuit of personal and professional development. Staff develop skills in data analysis, project management, strategic communication, stakeholder engagement, and leadership.

Advisory Services

Through the support of funders and stakeholders, NLF has grown and built a strong Advisory Services unit to support other organisations. In the coperate sector, NLF's data tools and predictive analytics capability help large companies/organisations access and track key indicators for the corporate social investment projects they have invested in.

This assists corporates and the NGOs they fund to effectively track progress, achieve greater impact, and measure a higher return on investment for their education-related CSI spend. NLF has also partnered with Data Innovators Group to scale its impact across development sectors globally.
